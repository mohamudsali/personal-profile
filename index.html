Mohamud Ali
Researcher
IBM Quantum Research

I am a Research Scientist on IBM’s Quantum Systems and Infrastructure team. I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering at the University of Minnesota–Twin Cities under the supervision of Prof. Kevin Cao. I also hold an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Northwestern University, Evanston, IL, and a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Minnesota–Twin Cities.

My Ph.D. research focuses on analog in-memory computing for AI, particularly at the intersection of AI systems and hardware-aware modeling. I am interested in developing error-resilient AI methods that account for noise and variability in computing platforms.

I have worked on optimizing advanced semiconductor nodes, including 2 nm and 7 nm. At IBM, I design analog and mixed-signal chips for quantum error correction, focusing on the design and testing of integrated circuits for qubit readout and control.

In my free time, you’ll find me betwixt and between—either playing soccer or running, whether in the jungle or on the treadmill. Oh, and not to mention, I’m a bit of a bookaholic too!
